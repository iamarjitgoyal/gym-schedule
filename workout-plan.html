⚠️
Arm Imbalance Correction Protocol
For all unilateral exercises (dumbbell curls, single-arm rows, dumbbell press, etc.) — always start with your weaker left arm and match that exact weight and reps on the right. Never let your right arm compensate for the left. Additionally, do 1 extra set for left arm only on bicep and tricep days until the gap closes (typically 6–8 weeks).

  • 12–15 rep range
  • Start imbalance protocol
  • Caloric surplus +300 kcal

  • 10–12 rep range
  • Track all weights used
  • Aim for +1–2 kg body weight
  • Sleep 7–8 hrs mandatory

Volume Surge
  • Add drop sets on isolations
  • 8–12 rep range
  • Slow eccentric (3 sec down)

  • Protein 2g per kg bodyweight

  • Superset isolation movements
  • 8–10 rep range

  • Deload in week 4
  • Assess transformation fully
  • Plan next 3-month cycle
Key Trainer Notes
🍗
Nutrition Strategy
Eat in a caloric surplus of 300–400 kcal/day. Target 2g of protein per kg body weight (~114g/day). Prioritize chicken, eggs, paneer, lentils, and Greek yogurt. Have a protein-rich meal within 45 minutes post-workout. Don't skip carbs — rice and oats fuel your lifts.
📈
Progressive Overload
Add 2.5 kg every 1–2 weeks on compound lifts when you can complete all sets with good form. For dumbbells, increase by the smallest increment available. This is the single most important factor in muscle growth — log every session.
😴
Recovery is Training
Muscles grow during rest, not during lifting. Aim for 7–8 hours of sleep every night — this is non-negotiable for your body weight goal. Keep Saturday as an active recovery walk (20–30 min). Overtraining will stall your gains faster than anything else.
🎯
Belly Fat Approach
You cannot spot-reduce fat, but as you build muscle and eat in a clean surplus, your body composition will shift noticeably. Heavy compound lifts (squats, deadlifts, bench) trigger growth hormone that helps metabolize belly fat. Ab work on Wed + Fri will strengthen and tighten the core.
🔥
Warm-Up Protocol
Never skip warm-up. Do 5–7 minutes of light cardio, then 2 warm-up sets (50–60% of working weight) for the first exercise of each session. For shoulders and legs, add dynamic stretching. Injury prevention is critical for consistent progress over 5 months.
💧
Hydration & Timing
Drink 3–4 liters of water daily. Have a pre-workout meal 1–1.5 hrs before training — rice + chicken or banana + peanut butter. Post-workout: fast protein within 45 min (whey shake or eggs). Creatine monohydrate 5g/day is the one supplement worth adding for your goal.
— Review and adjust weights every 4 weeks. Consult a physician before starting any new training program. TRANSFORM PROTOCOL
